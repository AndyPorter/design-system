What should we do next -

By its very nature a design system is opinionated. There is more than one way to solve a problem, even if there are best practices. Even following well proven patterns, the implementation of form and function can be tailored. To bring consistency there needs to be a set of 'best practice' and an opinionated set of advice, look and feel - what makes one organisations design system different from or consistent/symapthetic to anothers

Phase 1 - what might a useful design system be for us end Jan 2020

Exploring ideas of what could it contain, and how could it be structured, what would it take to make one, what is important first

Taking all the best ideas from exsiting GOV Design systems and some well known non-Gov organisations, and thinking about - if a design system helps you build things quickly with proven solutions, what could it contain?

This gave us knowledge of how we could more effectively collect and collate information - it needs to be structured and templatised - for both the reader and author understanding, and to efficiently build and maintain a system

We learnt about what designers think it could/should contain, and how we might organise and show patterns and advice meant for more than one channel (e.g. portal, gov.uk, and staff)
