Help out

Make it a whole practice effort. Our collective, authoritative, knowledge, made public.

At this point there is a lot that can be improved - we're in a phase of creating a body of agreed, proven advice. Feedback and comment on anything is welcome - most documents can be edited by everyone in the practice, the rest are comment only.

What is most needed

Documenting new designs

We took a snapshot of what's in production, over a few months. That's already out of date. We need to find a way to keep on top of whats been designed, why, how it meets users needs, what didn't work, what needs more evidence, etc, etc.

We've (in the design practice) not prescribed a standard way of documentation - either if we need to demonstrate at an assessment, or to help each other out - to save repeating work.

For the design system we are mainly interested in what finally goes into the service/product/system. Things you've tried inside the team, inside design, is of less interest - document all that in whatever way most works for you.

For a design system we need:

  • ongoing updates to 'whats in use'. We need more examples and: why its designed as it is, if it's different to GOV advice - how and why, how well it works (evidence, e.g. usability testing, analytics, copied from elsewhere in gov), what you'd like to learn next about it (more users, different context/users, etc)
  • if no document exists yet for your component, pattern, or the topic you think is needed - just start one. Either start or contact Andy Porter or Laura, to ask where to store this, or for a template
  • however you do it, do document your work, and we can use it within the practice and design system later

Evidence about designs

A good amount of usability testing goes on, before things are built. Less goes on after code is shipped and there isn't much detailed analytical evidence.

Designs could be brilliant - but we've no idea. We've all see our designs bomb once they meet a real user in a usability session.

We've no real idea how well whats currently been built works. This is slowing us down in saying - this advice is 'proven' - in this context with these users - copy it.

This is an area where we probably need the help of other practices. Talk to your user research colleagues, and performance analysts in your team - and together we can improve on what we currently know.

We need more evidence of:

  • how much usability testing, pre-production
  • usability testing post production
  • analytics
  • evidence from different types of users - especially those with accessibility needs
  • more evidence of context in which it works
  • where things don't work
  • saying 'We don't know how well this works' is really helpful, and describing what you would like to see done
  • what didn't work - designs we threw out

Evidence can be recorded in the 'whats in use' documents, or just add a link there to your own teams (or other practices) work.

Survey - design systems, what do you need?

We'd like to understand more about - what should a design system be, how does it help people, do we need one? What should we prioritise - meet peoples needs.

Non designers, and designers who didn't complete the first survey, let us know: if you use a design system, or would like one (no closing date yet; anonymous). Contact Andy Porter if any problem accessing.
