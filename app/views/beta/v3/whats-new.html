What's new here?

Introducing the Design system

We've started a Design System for HM Land Registry digital teams. To help teams better meet the needs of the users of services and systems, at a reduced cost. Higher quality, more quickly attained.

This is the start of: publishing a body of reviewed, agreed and evidenced advice and patterns, primarily for designers. The previous introduction (not widely shared) outlines where we were at in August.

Knowing what's what

The site will be the only link you need to find out: the latest advice to follow, how to contribute, whats going on with the design system and whats already been done. Work in the open.

Helping out

The system will be most successful if it's a whole (and cross) practice thing. There is no master blueprint of what that this all looks like which we are implementing piecemeal. This is an agile, iterative, approach toward some desired outcomes and goals, using principles like 'seek continuous feedback' and 'work in the open'.

Get involved or give feedback at any time, on anything (email Andy Porter and Laura Clifford). In these design system pages and documents we'll highlight where specific help is needed.

Hierarchy of advice

We've created a hierarchy of advice to follow when creating new services, products or systems, for GOV.UK services, professional users and staff.

All new work should follow this approach, not just greenfield services and systems.
