Design advice for all new work updated: 23 Sept 2020

This is a hierarchy of advice to follow when creating new services, products or systems, for GOV.UK services, professional users and staff

  1. start with GOV.UK design advice (this is well proven)
  2. use agreed HMLR Design advice (this is good enough to reuse, but needs more evidence)
  3. be influenced by current 'designs in production' (we've yet to establish how reusable this is)

Start at the top, and work down only if you can demonstrate that the advice above does not work.

Document designs when diverging from GOV.UK advice.

1. Start with GOV.UK advice

You should always start with GOV.UK patterns, components and advice if you’re looking at solving problems for users. Your user research will tell you if you need to think about adapting something. This is for all of the work we do - not just for GOV.UK hosted services.

This advice can be found in the:

  • GOV.UK Design system
  • GOV.UK Content design guide
  • GOV.UK A to Z style guide
  • GOV.UK Service Manual

2. HMLR advice you can follow now

When we feel like there is advice and solutions that are good enough to follow, they will live here.

We’ve stored solutions in Google docs up until now. We’d like feedback (email to Andy and Laura) on how you’d like to consume this type of information so you can reuse it in the future.

Current followable advice:

  • Welsh language translations for common service elements and HMLR terminology
  • approved definitions for tenure and register information
  • using acronyms and domain terminology

3. What’s in our production systems

The following designs are what we currently have in production. They can be helpful for new work when your circumstances differ from GOV patterns and advice.

You may find this collection useful to:

  • view how other services have tackled problems
  • generate ideas of how you could approach something in your service
  • find out the reasons why something has been developed the way it has

We need to keep documenting what is in use, why, and what evidence we have for its effectiveness.

Everyone can update these documents, if you are unsure, you can ask Andy and Laura.

How can I contribute?

Our next step for the design system is to publish more agreed (proven) advice - from the information collected in the Google docs.

The Google docs where the practice collaborates, records what we've done and learnt, and are the necessary body of work used to distill from the agreed, proven, reusable, advice.

They are open to all, jump in, and use the Google docs for what they are best at - collaborative documents - add what you know, comments, ask questions, make suggestions.

We’d really like you to:

  • add designs to the docs - what we have, what works and what doesn't, why; who for and context; evidence (saying I don't know is OK).
  • add comments to the docs. Did you try something and find out it didn’t work for your users? Do you have any more research or reasons why you did something?
  • let us know if there’s anything specific you’d like us to focus on next or prioritise

Document a pattern or component that’s not listed, has no link, or not sure how to contribute

email Andy or Laura, and we'll start a document for you.
